How placement schools working with initial teacher training (ITT) providers can claim funding to cover the costs associated with training a general mentor.

For the 2024 to 2025 school year, teachers who mentor a trainee teacher must complete up to 20 hours general mentor training with an ITT provider.

The general mentor training grant is to support the costs of having general mentors train to meet the new ITT quality requirements.

Read more about the changes to ITT.

{{ sectionTitles[0] }}

This funding is to help schools with the cost of covering teacher’s time to participate in mentor training. Where it is not possible to give the time-off timetable to the teacher for mentor training, the schools must give the teacher time-off timetable at another time or pass the funding to the teacher.

Schools can claim up to 20 hours per general mentor depending on your school’s region:

  • Inner London — up to £1,072
  • Outer London — up to £965
  • Fringe — up to £902
  • Rest of England — up to £876

Your maximum claim is based on the:

  • hours of training a teacher completed
  • cost incurred in covering the teacher’s time away from lessons
  • amount given to the teacher where time away from lessons was not possible

This is available to all schools that host trainee teachers, including independent schools.

The grant cannot be spent on:

  • teachers that train to become a general mentor without a trainee to mentor
  • travel
  • expenses
  • cover for teachers to train that is already paid for by someone else (for example, the ITT provider)

{{ sectionTitles[1] }}

To make a claim you will need:

  • the name of school you are claiming on behalf of
  • the name, birth date and teacher reference number (TRN) of teachers who took part in training
  • the hours of training completed by each teacher
  • the amount you want to claim for
  • confirmation that each teacher you are claiming for mentored at least one different trainee each

You will also need to keep and provide when requested evidence of:

  • the identity of staff attending general mentor training
  • timetables that show when a teacher attended training and needed cover, if applicable
  • timetables that show when a teacher was given time-off timetable at another time and needed cover, if applicable
  • transfer of money to the teacher where training was done in their own time, if applicable
  • hours of attending training. For example, provider certificates or registers

Claims will be paid between September and October 2025.

Payment for independent schools

If your school is not an academy or does not belong to a local authority, you will need to provide payment information. How to provide information about your banking and payments.

{{ sectionTitles[2] }}

Funding is to support all time teachers spend training. Including face-to-face training with the provider and learning mentor does on their own.

Each general mentor must mentor at least 1 trainee each in 2024/25. Only trainees that start in or after the 2024/25 school year count towards this. For example, if a trainee had 2 general mentors and neither of those mentors worked with another trainee who started in 2024/25. The school could only claim for 1 general mentor’s training.

This grant is also subject to the additional conditions set out in the DfE’s standard grant terms and conditions.

If an ITT trainee withdraws or defers:

  • before a teacher starts their general mentor training, the school cannot claim any funding
  • after a teacher has started their general mentor training, the school can claim up to the full amount

{{ sectionTitles[3] }}

Schools who choose to spend grant funding on ineligible activities will be liable for a grant clawback and this could impact their eligibility for any future grants.
