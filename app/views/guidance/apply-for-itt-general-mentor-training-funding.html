For the 2024 to 2025 academic year all teachers who mentor a trainee during their ITT placement must have completed 20 hours of general mentor training with an ITT provider.

Read more about ITT mentor training.

Schools can claim funding for the time a teacher takes to train.

{{ sectionTitles[1] }}

Funding is to support schools with general mentor training. This is for any time spent training, not only face-to-face with ITT providers.

Funding is adjusted by region. For each mentor schools can claim:

  • Inner London — up to £1,072
  • Outer London — up to £965
  • Fringe — up to £902
  • Rest of England — up to £876

This is available to all schools that host trainees, including independent schools.

What is not covered by the funding

  • teachers that train to become a general mentor without a trainee to mentor
  • travel time or expenses

What happens if a trainee withdraws or defers

If an ITT trainee withdraws or defers:

  • before a teacher starts their general mentor training, the school cannot claim any funding
  • after a teacher has started their general mentor training, the school can claim up to the full amount
