For the 2024 to 2025 school year, teachers who mentor a trainee teacher must complete up to 20 hours of general mentor training with an ITT provider.

Read more about general ITT mentor training .

This funding is to help schools with the cost of covering teacher’s time to participate in mentor training.

Where it’s not possible to give the time-off timetable to the teacher for mentor training, the schools must give the teacher time-off timetable at another time or pass the funding to the teacher.

Before you start

{{ sectionTitles[0] }}

Funding is to support schools with general mentor training. This is for any time spent training, not only face-to-face with ITT providers.

Each general mentor must mentor at least 1 trainee each. For example, if a trainee had 2 general mentors and neither of those mentors worked with another trainee. The school could only claim for 1 general mentor’s training.

Funding is adjusted by region. For each general mentor, schools can claim:

  • Inner London — up to £1,072
  • Outer London — up to £965
  • Fringe — up to £902
  • Rest of England — up to £876

Your maximum claim based on the:

  • hours of training a teacher completed
  • cost incurred in covering the teacher’s time away from lessons
  • amount given to the teacher where time away from lessons was not possible

This is available to all schools that host trainee teachers, including independent schools.

What is not covered by the funding

  • teachers that train to become a general mentor without a trainee to mentor
  • travel time or expenses

What happens if a trainee withdraws or defers

If an ITT trainee withdraws or defers:

  • before a teacher starts their general mentor training, the school cannot claim any funding
  • after a teacher has started their general mentor training, the school can claim up to the full amount

  • transfer of money to the teacher where training was done in their own time, if applicable
  • hours of training attended. For example, provider’s certificates of completion or a register of attendance

Applications for ITT general mentor funding must be made by 31 August 2025.
