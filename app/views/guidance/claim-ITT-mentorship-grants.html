Get top-up payments for lesson cover while your teachers take part in initial teacher training (ITT) mentor training.

From: Department for Education

Published 1 September 2023

Get emails about this page

Applies to England

{% set sections = [ { title: 'Overview', id: '', contentHtml: '

For the 2024 to 2025 academic year all teachers who mentor a trainee during their ITT placement must have completed 20 hours of mentor training with an ITT provider.

Read more about ITT mentor training.

Schools who need lesson cover while teachers complete the 20 hours of mentor training can claim a top-up payment from the DfE.

' }, { title: 'What you can claim', id: '', contentHtml: '

Schools can claim up to £644 per mentor for lesson cover while teachers train to be a mentor. This is for any training, not only time spent in the classroom with an ITT provider.

This is available to all schools that host mentors, including independent schools.

' }, { title: 'What you cannot claim for', id: '', contentHtml: '
  • time covered that is already paid for by the ITT provider
  • training time when the school did not need cover
  • teachers that trained to be a mentor but did not mentor a trainee
  • lead mentor training time
' }, { title: 'How to claim', id: '', contentHtml: '

After all your mentors have completed their training, email ITTReform.Funding@education.gov.uk with:

  • the school’s name and URN
  • the hours of cover so your teachers could train, that is not already paid for by the ITT provider
  • the number of teachers who took part in mentor training
  • confirmation that each teacher you’re claiming for mentored at least one different trainee each

All claims must be made by 31 October 2025.

' }, { title: 'What happens next', id: '', contentHtml: '

Claims will be paid within 1 month of the claim being made.

We will collect actual cost of class room cover as part of the end of financial year assurance processes in March 2026.

' } ] %}
