This is a funding is available to providers to support the costs associated with introducing the lead mentor role. This funding is designed to help providers with the cost for placement schools or themselves of the staff time needed to cover these new lead mentor duties.

Read more about the duties and responsibilities of a lead mentor in the ITT Criteria 2024 to 2025.

{{ sectionTitles[0] }}

The minimum expectation is that a lead mentor will spend 30 hours in training activities during the 2024 to 2025 academic year.

As set out in C2.3 of the ITT Criteria 2024 to 2025, you must be able to demonstrate that each of your lead mentors have been trained in all areas of your 30 hour lead mentor training curriculum.

To prevent unnecessary repetition of training, you may take into account lead mentors’ prior learning. For example, a lead mentor that has recently completed a training course covering skills included in the lead mentor training curriculum or that has had close involvement in designing the mentor (or trainee) training curricula may not be required to undergo the full 30 hours of training.

Mentors with significant previous experience of mentoring or otherwise developing others may also be able to evidence understanding of certain aspects of the provider’s mentor training curriculum, in which case they may be exempted from training on these aspects. This should not be based on a self-audit by the lead mentor. Providers are expected to have examples of how the mentor has frequently made use of the relevant mentoring approach in recent years.

{{ sectionTitles[1] }}

For every 25 trainees training at time of the ITT census in October 2024, providers can claim up to £9,514.

How to calculate the trainee to lead mentor ratio

There is not a required ratio for lead mentors to trainees. For the purpose of calculating funding, providers will be able to claim funding for 1 lead mentor to a minimum of 25 trainees.

All trainees on routes that lead to QTS (except Assessment only) included in the October 2024 ITT census count towards this ratio. Including trainees on undergraduate and part time trainees.

Providers that have fewer than 25 trainees can claim up to £9,514.

Providers must pass funding onto schools if the lead mentor is employed by a school and taking time away from their school activities.

Funding is available for all activity from 1 August 2024 to 31 July 2025.

{{ sectionTitles[2] }}

The funding is available to providers who:

  • have passed stage 2 of the accreditation process
  • provide evidence of costs when claiming funding
  • sign the grant funding agreement

{{ sectionTitles[3] }}

When you claim funding you will need to provide evidence of the cost of lead mentor training time.

You must keep a record of time spent on mentoring activity and a log of the individuals they have supported.

Further evidence of activity can include:

  • timetable evidence which shows when a mentor attended training, provided training or spent time with general mentors
  • contracts of staff employed to fulfil the lead mentor role
  • transfer of funds to schools, where appropriate

As part of the grant funding agreement, the DfE will request evidence from a sample of ITT providers in February 2025. You will have 30 days to submit evidence when requested.
