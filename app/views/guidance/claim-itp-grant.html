This funding stream is to help providers and schools with the costs of delivering the new intensive training and practice requirement. This funding is designed to support the costs of both group and individual expert time as well as the administrative costs of organising this activity.

{{ sectionTitles[0] }}

Postgraduate trainees

Up to £204 per trainee for:

  • 4 weeks of training during the 2024 to 2025 academic year
  • trainees who started in the 2024 to 2025 academic year
  • trainees registered at the time of the ITT October census 2024

Undergraduate trainees

Up to £298 per trainee for:

  • up to 6 weeks of training during the 2024 to 2025 academic year
  • trainees who started in the 2024 to 2025 academic year
  • trainees registered at the time of the ITT October census 2024

Providers must prioritise sharing this funding with placement schools where activity is school-based or utilises school-based staff.

{{ sectionTitles[1] }}

The funding is available to providers who:

  • have passed stage 2 of the accreditation process
  • provide evidence of costs when claiming funding
  • sign the grant funding agreement

Providers must prioritise sharing this funding with placement schools where activity is school-based or utilises school-based staff.

Funding is available for all activity from 1 April 2024 to 31 July 2025.

{{ sectionTitles[2] }}

When you claim funding you will need to provide evidence of the cost of intensive training and practice.

The evidence you need to provide is:

  • transfer of funds to schools
  • classroom attendance

Types of evidence can be:

  • contracts of staff employed to provide training
  • registers
  • timetable evidence

As part of the grant funding agreement, the DfE will request evidence from a sample of ITT providers during the 2024 to 2025 academic year. You will have 30 days to submit evidence when requested.
